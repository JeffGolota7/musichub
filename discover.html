YBN Cordae

Cordae Amari Dunston (born August 26, 1997), known professionally as YBN Cordae, is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter. Dunston began rapping at a young age under the stage name Entendre. He released three mixtapes under this name: Anxiety (2014), I'm So Anxious (2016), and I'm So Anonymous (2017) before becoming a member of the YBN collective in 2018. Dunston subsequently gained popularity by releasing remixes of popular songs, such as "My Name Is" by Eminem, and "Old Ni**as" which was a response to the song "1985" by J. Cole. Videos to both remixes were uploaded to the WorldStarHipHop's YouTube channel, which led to an immense and immediate positive response from both mainstream media and the online rap community.[3] His debut studio album, The Lost Boy was released on July 26, 2019[4] and received a nomination for Best Rap Album at the 62nd Grammy Awards.[5]

Aaron May

At such a young age, Aaron May, 17 is coming into his music career with his own sound. From Houston, Texas, Aaron grew to fame with his release of his first single "Ride" in June of 2018. Through Aaron's own promotions of his music and with the help of his hometown friends, "Ride" received over 12,000 views on YouTube. Only at the start of his career, Aaron has already released his first mixtape, Chase. In January 2019 Aaron released a second single called "Let Go" which received over 558,000 views on YouTube and which he included in his 2019 mixtape, Chase. His biggest role model is J.Cole, whom he is often compared to for his strong lyricism that he uses to relate to his fans, and his flow

AK

Austin Kassabian is a young rapper from New Jersey. The 20-year-old emcee has amassed enormous streaming totals online: more than 100 million plays on YouTube and 15 million listens on Spotify ever since his laser-aimed remix of Desiigner’s mega-hit “Panda” went viral in 2016 (when Kassabian was 16) and scored a cool 30 million plays on its own. The online success, bolstered by consistent releases of his own fiery original tunes, earned Kassabian notice from LH7 Management’s Brian Teefey — pop superstar Selena Gomez’s stepfather and former manager.
